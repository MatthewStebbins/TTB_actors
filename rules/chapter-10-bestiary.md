# Chapter 10: Bestiary

## Overview

The Bestiary provides Fatemasters with stat blocks for characters and creatures from every faction and walk of life in Malifaux. Stat blocks may be adjusted — increasing a character's Rank Value by 2 points typically creates an "elite" version. Exercise caution when raising any character above Henchman rank.

Additional stat blocks can be found in supplements such as *Into the Steam*, *Under Quarantine*, and *Into the Bayou*.

---

## Stat Block Format Reference

Each stat block uses the following structure:

- **Aspects:** Might, Grace, Speed, Resilience, Charm, Cunning, Intellect, Tenacity (range −5 to 5)
- **Rank:** Rank Title (Rank Value), plus character type tags (Living/Undead/Construct, faction, subtype)
- **Derived Stats:** Defense (Df), Walk, Height, Initiative, Willpower (Wp), Charge, Wounds
  - *Parenthetical values* = base stat + Rank Value (e.g., Df 4 (9) for a Minion 5 = 4+5)
- **Skills:** Skill Name Rank. A suffix letter indicates the Aspect used: no suffix = default Aspect pairing; M = Might, G = Grace, etc.
- **Abilities:** Passive or triggered special rules.
- **Actions:** Format: `(AP cost) Name (Skill) | AV: X (X+RV) | Rg: y/z/N | Resist: Df/Wp`
  - Damage listed as Weak/Moderate/Severe (e.g., 2/3/4)
  - Triggers listed as: `[Suit] Trigger Name: Effect`
  - Weapon properties listed inline (Capacity N, Reload N, etc.)

### Hunter

Originally designed to track escaped convicts, smugglers, and bandits, Hunter constructs feature segmented bodies and specially sprung legs for agility and stealth, plus steel claws for close combat. Their chain harpoons can spear fleeing targets at range and instantly retract to yank the victim back toward the machine. Developed in conjunction with the Miners and Steamfitters Union, each Hunter commissioned requires a significant licensing fee — a restriction that led the Guild to place quarterly commission limits and consolidate most Hunters under the Amalgamation Office.

### Riotbreaker

A variation on the Guardian design, Riotbreakers are walking shieldwalls optimized for crowd control. Their shotguns are fired downward to ricochet off the ground — reducing shot velocity to nonlethal levels while increasing spread. Pneumatic entrenching spikes anchor them in place when stationary, making them nearly impossible to shove aside. Hated by the public — largely due to Union propaganda depicting them shooting unarmed strikers — the Guild has reduced their deployment in recent years, fielding them only when deemed absolutely necessary.

### Death Marshal

One of the few Guild Special Divisions genuinely appreciated by Malifaux's citizens, the Death Marshals exist for a single purpose: locate and eliminate Resurrectionists and their undead creations. Trained in both combat and necromancy (though forbidden from creating undead), Death Marshals are also skilled trackers and interrogators. Their necromantic training leaves their bodies withered and decaying — the iconic high collars and wide-brimmed hats originally concealed their grim appearances before becoming official uniform elements. Their signature tactic: trap the controlling Resurrectionist alive in their pine box coffin and return it for interrogation.

Violent disagreements between Exorcists of different faiths are common, but their results are hard to argue with.

### Sorrow

Sorrows are the physical manifestations of their namesake: attracted to despondency, they serve only to increase melancholy and drive those around them to despair. It is theorized that the presence of these depressing spirits is the reason why Malifaux is such a bleak place, as even the most cheerful person will find themselves overcome with self-loathing and inadequacy in their presence.

It is from this spiral of self-loathing that Sorrows derive their sustenance. The influence of one Sorrow often attracts more of their kind, which in turn drives the helpless victim into even darker depths of depression, which then attracts more Sorrows in a self-perpetuating spiral of despair.

While Sorrows are not particularly dangerous in physical combat, they can quickly sap the spirit of even the strongest opponent. Mustering the resolve to attack one can be a problem — their victims often see little reason to raise their sword and instead prefer to slump to the ground listlessly as the Sorrow slowly feeds on their ebbing life.

### Terror Tot

Terror Tots are the youngest of the Nephilim race, but their small size belies their ruthlessness. Though they cannot yet fly, they do not lack mobility — Terror Tots use their horse-like hooves to dig into the ground and spring at their enemies. When in a hunting pack, they alternate between attacking and withdrawing to keep their prey disoriented.

Upon killing their prey, Terror Tots feed upon the corpse. If a Terror Tot consumes enough blood, its body undergoes a sudden and violent transformation into a ravenous Young Nephilim.

The Black Blood of the Nephilim is extremely dangerous to other creatures. Just a few drops on living flesh can eat through to the bone, and it's acidic enough to damage constructs as well. Worse yet, repeated exposure has a warping effect, gradually transforming the infected into a Nephilim in both body and mind.
