# Chapter 10: Bestiary

## Overview

The Bestiary provides Fatemasters with stat blocks for characters and creatures from every faction and walk of life in Malifaux. Stat blocks may be adjusted — increasing a character's Rank Value by 2 points typically creates an "elite" version. Exercise caution when raising any character above Henchman rank.

Additional stat blocks can be found in supplements such as *Into the Steam*, *Under Quarantine*, and *Into the Bayou*.

---

## Stat Block Format Reference

Each stat block uses the following structure:

- **Aspects:** Might, Grace, Speed, Resilience, Charm, Cunning, Intellect, Tenacity (range −5 to 5)
- **Rank:** Rank Title (Rank Value), plus character type tags (Living/Undead/Construct, faction, subtype)
- **Derived Stats:** Defense (Df), Walk, Height, Initiative, Willpower (Wp), Charge, Wounds
  - *Parenthetical values* = base stat + Rank Value (e.g., Df 4 (9) for a Minion 5 = 4+5)
- **Skills:** Skill Name Rank. A suffix letter indicates the Aspect used: no suffix = default Aspect pairing; M = Might, G = Grace, etc.
- **Abilities:** Passive or triggered special rules.
- **Actions:** Format: `(AP cost) Name (Skill) | AV: X (X+RV) | Rg: y/z/N | Resist: Df/Wp`
  - Damage listed as Weak/Moderate/Severe (e.g., 2/3/4)
  - Triggers listed as: `[Suit] Trigger Name: Effect`
  - Weapon properties listed inline (Capacity N, Reload N, etc.)

## Guild

### Guild Huntmaster & Guard Dog

Guard dogs were among the first animals brought to Malifaux by the Guild, valued for their ability to detect Neverborn by scent and serve as cheap sentries. Guild Huntmasters raise and supervise these animals, training them to attack trespassers on sight while allowing Guild-uniformed personnel to pass freely.

### Guild Guardsman

The Guild Guard is the constabulary arm of the Guild, responsible for law enforcement and protecting Guild strongholds. Despite Guild propaganda portraying them as valiant protectors, guardsmen are frequently underpaid and susceptible to bribery — a practice so normalized that many Malifaux residents offer bribes proactively.

### Guild Rifleman

Elite marksmen transferred from the Guild Guard after demonstrating exceptional marksmanship and discipline. Riflemen undergo extensive training in target selection, battlefield awareness, and long-range shooting. Despite their stated defensive role, the Guild frequently uses them for intimidation — suppressing Union strikes and deterring prison escapes. A select few are recruited by the Elite Division as covert assassins, paid generously for their silence and discretion, though such "distinguished agents" rarely enjoy a long retirement.
